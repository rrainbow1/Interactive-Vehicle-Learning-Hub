Animation: the red cursor moves across the pressure waveform while the piston, crankshaft and valves move through the corresponding 720° four-stroke cycle.
Pressure scale: the known-good waveform is scaled to a representative petrol cranking peak of about 12 bar(g) / 174 psi. Negative values represent pressure below atmospheric. Actual healthy compression varies by engine, cranking speed, temperature, altitude and test setup; manufacturer or known-good data must be used for real diagnosis.
Note: this is a teaching simulation. Actual pressure values and valve opening/closing points vary by engine.
Valve overlap
Valve overlap occurs around the TDC transition between the exhaust and intake strokes, when the exhaust valve has not fully closed and the inlet valve has already started to open. This helps scavenging and cylinder filling on many engines.

Purple pins on the scope: the two pins show the start and end of the overlap window. The shaded area between them represents the period when both valves are open. This simulator uses approximately 340°–380° as a clear teaching example around 360° TDC; actual valve opening, closing and overlap angles must be taken from the engine manufacturer's data.
