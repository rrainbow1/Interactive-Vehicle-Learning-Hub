Degree reference: The green dashed line is the Cylinder 1 crankshaft TDC datum at 360° in this 720° cycle. The green point on the cam channel is the corresponding mechanical cam timing datum, not a cam-sensor switching edge. The white dashed line is the expected known-good cam sensor edge at 405° CA (45° after the TDC datum in this generic teaching model). The red line is the actual cam edge. The red bracket measures the difference between expected and actual cam position in crankshaft degrees (°CA). Divide °CA by 2 for equivalent physical camshaft rotation.

Known good: fixed, repeatable cam/crank relationship.
Generic teaching model: This uses a representative 60-2 digital crank pattern, a 0–5 V Hall-style cam signal and a 0–12 V injector command/reference pulse. The waveform positions are now mathematically tied to the voltage scales shown beside each channel. A real engine must always be compared with manufacturer data or a known-good waveform for that exact engine. In this model all displays use one shared crank-angle model. Cylinder 1 TDC is 360° CA; the generic known-good cam correlation edge is 405° CA. A one-tooth error on the representative 36-tooth cam sprocket is 10° physical cam rotation = 20° crank-angle correlation error. The scope phase, degree bracket and timing-cover sprocket displacement are mathematically linked.
